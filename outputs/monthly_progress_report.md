**Overall Progress**

As of today, 25 submissions have come in from 20 departments. 14 are approved (56%) and 11 are still pending (44%). Ten departments are fully cleared; the other ten have at least one submission waiting on a correction.

**Top Compliance Issues**

Three issues account for most of the rejections, and all three have a 100% non-response rate after follow-up, which is the part worth paying attention to:

1. **Missing data owner** (4 submissions) - departments are leaving this field blank rather than getting it wrong, which suggests either the form isn't clear about who should be named here, or the departments genuinely don't have a designated owner yet.
2. **DPDP flag inconsistent with classification** (4 submissions) - personal data marked "Yes" but classification left as Public or blank. This one concerns me a bit more than the others, since it points to departments not fully understanding the DPDP classification rules rather than just rushing the form.
3. **Classification missing or invalid** (2 submissions) - similar root cause to #2, likely a training gap rather than carelessness.

Two smaller issues - missing descriptions and missing record counts - are also showing up, but departments are actually responding to those follow-ups, so they look more like oversight than a knowledge gap.

**Recommended Actions (Next 2 Weeks)**

2. Run a short clarification note (or a quick session) on the DPDP classification rules specifically, since that's the issue most departments seem to be getting wrong rather than just missing.
3. Add a mandatory "data owner" field at the point of submission so it can't be left blank in the first place - this alone would resolve 4 of the 11 pending cases.
